Tripolarity

Three forces live within you. Understanding them is the first step toward freedom.

You have felt it — the pull between who society wants you to be and something deeper, wilder, older. This is not a flaw. It is the architecture of your psyche: three forces in constant dialogue. When one dominates, you suffer. When all three are heard, you become whole.

This is not a religion, not a doctrine. It is a mirror. Read slowly. Recognize yourself.

🔥

The Abysmal

Deep in your nervous system lives an ancient inheritance — millions of years of survival encoded in your instincts. This is the Abysmal: your animal core. It is the adrenaline before a fight, the electricity of desire, the territorial vigilance that scans every room you enter. It is not evil. It is the raw engine of life itself.

Every emotion you feel has roots here: anger descends from territorial defense. Fear is the sentinel that kept your ancestors alive. Lust is the reproductive drive that propagated the species. Jealousy guarded pair bonds. Pack loyalty — that fierce belonging you feel with close friends — mirrors the social structure of wolves and primates.

And here is the truth that may unsettle you: it is not only rage, fear, and desire that live here. Love does too. Watch any mammal — a dog's devotion to its owner, a mother cat's fierce protection of her kittens, the grief of an elephant at a companion's death. Love, care, attachment, the willingness to die for your young — these are not gifts from above. They are ancient biological forces, forged in the same furnace as aggression. Religion and ideology claim love as celestial, as divine — but this is a comfortable misattribution. Your love is powerful precisely because it is primal. What the Celestial truly adds is not love itself, but awareness within love — the difference between loving blindly and loving consciously.

Society tells you these forces are dangerous, shameful, sick. It medicates them, moralizes them, buries them under rules. But repression does not destroy energy — it redirects it. Suppressed rage becomes chronic anxiety. Denied desire becomes obsession. Buried wildness erupts as depression, addiction, or sudden violence.

The Abysmal is not your enemy. It is your power source — but it needs direction, not a cage.

The Telluric

The Telluric is the earth-bound self: the builder, the worker, the pragmatist. It is the force that constructs houses, earns wages, follows schedules, and obeys traffic lights. Society adores this pole because it produces. Economies run on Telluric energy. Schools cultivate it. Corporations harvest it.

In moderation, the Telluric is essential — it gives structure, discipline, and material security. You need it to feed yourself, to shelter those you love, to participate in the world. Without it, ideas remain dreams and instincts have no vessel.

But our era has made a religion of materialism. The modern world is a Telluric exacerbation: work harder, produce more, consume endlessly, define your worth by output. When this pole dominates unchecked, life becomes a conveyor belt — efficient but meaningless. You wake, commute, perform, consume, sleep, repeat. The existential void is not a mystery; it is the symptom of a life reduced to a single frequency.

The Telluric without the other two poles produces the walking dead: people who have everything and feel nothing.

Strengths
Discipline, reliability, building capacity, pragmatism, material stability
Traps
Workaholism, meaningless routine, identity through consumption, emotional numbness
✦

The Celestial

The Celestial is the missing third — the force that neither religion nor materialism has fully claimed. It is your capacity to step back, observe yourself from above, and ask: Why am I doing this? What does it mean? Who am I beyond my urges and my routines?

This is not about a god in the sky or a church on Sunday. The Celestial is meaningful detachment: the ability to see your own patterns without drowning in them. It is critical thinking applied to yourself. It is the philosopher's stance — not cold intellectualism, but a warm, honest audit of your inner world.

When the Celestial is active, you can feel rage without becoming rage. You can work without becoming a machine. You can desire without being enslaved. It is the inner observer — the part of you that watches the animal and the worker and says: I see you both. I honor you both. But I am the one who decides.

The Celestial is where aspiration lives — not ambition for things, but aspiration toward becoming. Spiritual evolution not as dogma, but as the continuous, free-thinking expansion of consciousness. Personal development not as a market product, but as the authentic unfolding of who you already are.

Expressions
Self-awareness, holistic thinking, creative vision, compassion, inner sovereignty
Enemies
Dogma, commercial pseudo-spirituality, intellectual arrogance, spiritual bypassing

The Path to Balance

The goal is not to kill the animal within, nor to surrender to the machine. It is to awaken the observer who harmonizes both.

Integration is not a destination — it is a daily practice. You will never perfectly balance the three forces, and that is fine. What matters is awareness: knowing which pole is speaking at any given moment, and choosing your response instead of being chosen by it.

Observe without judging
When rage, desire, or restlessness surges — pause. Do not suppress it. Do not act on it blindly. Ask it: what do you need? The Abysmal speaks in sensations, not words. Learn its language.
Question the routine
Ask yourself regularly: am I working toward something meaningful, or just filling time? The Telluric disguises fear as productivity. Busyness is not purpose.
Create something
Write, draw, build, play music — anything that transforms inner energy into outer form. Creation is where the Abysmal and Celestial meet. It does not matter if it is good. What matters is that it is yours.
Seek silence
Not meditation apps or guided retreats — real silence. A mountain, a forest, a dark room at 3 AM. The Celestial speaks in whispers. You cannot hear it over the noise of a screen-saturated life.
Walk in nature
Your body remembers what your mind has forgotten. Earth under your feet, wind on your skin, the rhythm of walking — these reconnect the three poles faster than any book or theory.

The Social Archetypes

Look around you. Most people are trapped in one configuration of the three forces. Recognizing the patterns is not about judging others — it is about recognizing yourself.

🐑
The Sheep
Fully domesticated — but still animal. Pushed toward a Telluric existence: work, produce, consume, repeat. The Abysmal is not suppressed but managed through system-approved release valves — pornography channels desire, stadiums let tribal aggression roar within safe boundaries, war spectacles feed the predatory instinct vicariously. The Celestial is replaced with controlled substitutes — dogma, ideology, trends, pre-packaged meaning. All three poles fenced. The herd grazes contentedly, unaware that even its wildness has been domesticated. Comfortable but asleep.
🐕
The Shepherd's Dogs
Guardians of the system. They not only follow rules but enforce them. They believe order equals virtue. Their Abysmal is channeled into policing others.
🐺
The Internal Predators
Leaders who unconsciously exploit their tripolarity for personal gain. They manipulate others' fears and desires without having integrated their own. Power without wisdom.
✦
The Awakened
Those who have understood the three forces and found their dynamic balance. They neither dominate nor submit. They live with purpose, intensity, and inner freedom. This is what you can become.

The world is full of those who follow, those who guard, and those who exploit. But there are also those who see. You are reading this because something in you already knows. Trust that knowing.
