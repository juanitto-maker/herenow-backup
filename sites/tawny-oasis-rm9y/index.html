System Architecture
Universal 5-Layer Sandwich

Every panel variant shares the same structural logic — a modular sandwich where each layer can be swapped independently. The core and fill materials are interchangeable, keeping the system adaptable to whatever recycled materials are locally available.

1

Outer Shell (toward roof)

Waterproof + reflective barrier. Double cardboard with aluminum foil, or Tetrapak panels overlapped like tiles. ~1.2 cm

2

Insulation Core (interchangeable)

The modular heart — egg cartons, Tetrapak cells, jute pouches, PET bottles, or tin cans. Filled with perlite, crushed cork, or other fill. 3–4 cm

3

PCM Thermal Buffer (the magic)

Vacuum-sealed bags of paraffin or beeswax (melting point 25–30°C). Absorbs heat by day, releases warmth at night. ~200 kJ/m²

4

Inner Structural Layer

Double-layered cardboard base forming the rigid sandwich bottom. ~1.2 cm

5

Interior Finish (visible)

Craft paper, jute fabric, linen, or limewash-painted cardboard. Rustic Andalusian aesthetic. Breathable.

Each 1×1 m panel (~8 cm thick) rests on 3 parallel caña beams (30–35 mm ⌀), spaced ~40 cm apart.
Supported by locally harvested reed — cost-free if foraged.
Core Modules
5 Interchangeable Core Variations

The insulation core is the most adaptable layer. Use whichever recycled container is most abundant in your area.

🥚 Original

V1 — Egg Carton

Classic approach. Egg carton cells filled with perlite. Each cup traps air + mineral insulation. Widely available but inconsistent sizing and moisture-vulnerable.

Sourcing: Free from shops, restaurants
Moisture resistance: Low — needs foil barrier
Core: ~€0 + fill
🧃 ★ Best Option

V2 — Tetrapak Pairs

Cut one end off a Tetrapak box, fill with perlite, match open-to-open with another box. Creates a fully sealed, waterproof cell with built-in aluminum lining. Scalable into panel modules.

Sourcing: Abundant — bars, schools, recycling points
Moisture resistance: Excellent — PE + aluminum built-in
Bonus: Internal radiant micro-barriers
Core: ~€0 + fill
🪢 Eco-Premium

V3 — Jute Bag Quilted

Jute fabric stitched or tied into a waffle grid of pouches, each pocket filled with perlite or crushed cork. Breathable, fire-resistant when lime-treated, beautiful natural texture.

Sourcing: Coffee sacks, agricultural supply
Moisture resistance: Medium — breathable not waterproof
Bonus: Doubles as interior finish layer
Core: ~€0.50/m² + fill
🍶 Free Material

V4 — PET Bottles

Plastic bottles cut to 4 cm height, filled with perlite, paired mouth-to-mouth like Tetrapak. Lightweight, waterproof, and practically infinite supply from any recycling bin.

Sourcing: Everywhere — ultimate waste material
Moisture resistance: Excellent — plastic shell
Note: Irregular shapes = needs careful packing
Core: ~€0 + fill
🥫 Fireproof

V5 — Tin Cans

Sardine, tuna, and food tins cleaned and filled with perlite. Completely fireproof, rigid, and durable. Heavier than other options but indestructible. Metal conducts, so needs air gaps.

Sourcing: Free from restaurants, households
Moisture resistance: Excellent (if no rust)
Note: Heavier — may need extra caña support
Core: ~€0 + fill

Bonding
4 Natural Eco-Glues

No PVA needed. Each joint type gets its own purpose-made natural adhesive — all from locally available ingredients.

🧱 Lime–Casein

1 part curdled milk + 2 parts hydrated lime + water. Hardens into calcium caseinate — like stone.
Best for → Cardboard lamination, structural seams

🔥 Rosin–Wax–Shellac

3 parts pine rosin + 1 part beeswax + ½ part shellac. Melt, brush on, press while warm. Waterproof when cool.
Best for → Foil seams, waterproof bonds

🌳 Tannin–Lime

1 part strong oak/tea tannin solution + 1 part lime putty. Forms calcium-tannate cement — waterproof & hard.
Best for → Caña joints, corner reinforcement

🪴 Plant Resin + Oil

2 parts pine resin + 1 part linseed oil + ¼ part wood ash. Cook slowly until honey-like. Flexible & durable.
Best for → Flexible bonds, edge sealing
