TOP — Tessera Open Protocol

Abstract

A protocol, not a state

TOP is an open, federated protocol that enables individuals and AI agents to coordinate, transact, and govern their shared agreements — without territory, violence, or centralized authority. Like HTTP enables the web without any single entity owning it, TOP enables voluntary coordination without any single broker, server, or founder controlling it.

It combines three independent, composable layers — identity, coordination, and governance — into a system where participants own their credentials, choose their brokers, and exit freely at any time.

The paradox at the core of TOP:
The moment it tries to be a state, it fails.
The moment it stops trying — it may succeed.
The Problem

What's broken — and for whom

Legacy states evolved to control violence, taxation, scarce resources, and population movement. In a digital age, three of those four are weakening. Yet the infrastructure of governance remains territorial, coercive, and slow.

The people falling through the cracks aren't radicals. They're freelancers who can't get paid across borders, refugees who can't prove their identity, AI operators who absorb unlimited liability, nomads without a legal home, and cooperatives that can't sign contracts.

Prior Art

What already exists — and why TOP is different

The Network State (Balaji Srinivasan)

Balaji's model starts in the cloud but aims for land, diplomatic recognition, and sovereignty. It requires a recognized founder, crowdfunds physical territory, and gradually becomes a traditional state. TOP deliberately rejects all of this. No land. No sovereignty. No founder with special powers. Just a protocol.

Mutual aid platforms

Existing tools (Zelos, RubyForGood, Brightest) are essentially volunteer dispatch spreadsheets. They're human-coordinated, public-facing, and charity-framed. They require people to declare need publicly. TOP's AI-mediated routing preserves dignity: people express constraints, not poverty. The AI absorbs the social awkwardness.

DAOs and crypto governance

DAOs provide on-chain voting but lack identity, arbitration, and real-world enforceability. They also suffer from plutocratic governance (more tokens = more power). TOP separates governance from wealth. Reputation is contextual, non-transferable, and decaying.

TOP doesn't replace your country.
It replaces its worst parts.
Core Principles

The rules that can't be broken

Non-territorial

No land claims, no borders, no physical jurisdiction. Land makes you visible, vulnerable, and coercible. TOP exists entirely as protocol.

🚪

Absolute exit

Any participant can leave at any time, taking their attestations with them. No penalties, no delays, no stigma. If you can't leave, it's a prison.

🔀

No single entity controls matching, identity, or governance. Multiple competing brokers. Multiple arbitration providers. Like email servers — not like Facebook.

🧩

🤝

Dignity by design

No public feeds, no poverty labels, no virtue metrics. People express constraints and capabilities, never status. The AI absorbs the awkwardness of asymmetry.

🍴

Right to fork

The protocol, the code, and the governance model can be cloned and reused by anyone. Continuity is optional; freedom is mandatory.

Architecture

Three layers, ruthlessly separated

TOP's strength comes from keeping these layers independent. Someone can use just the identity layer without buying into governance. Adoption becomes modular — like how you can use email without using the web.

Layer 1
Identity Protocol
A citizen generates a cryptographic keypair — that's their identity. Over time, they accumulate attestations: signed receipts proving contracts completed, resources contributed, duration of activity. Attestations are held by the citizen, not stored centrally. No registry to hack. No database to subpoena.
Portable · Self-sovereign · No lock-in
Layer 2
Broker Protocol
Brokers are AI-powered matching nodes that accept intents, hold a local knowledge graph, and produce routings. They speak a common protocol for exchanging anonymized demand/supply signals without revealing citizen identities. Citizens only talk to their own broker. Brokers can specialize: food surplus, micro-contracts, investment matching. Anyone can run one.
Federated · Specialized · Replaceable
Layer 3
Governance Protocol
Arbitration, contracts, reputation, and dispute resolution — also federated. Multiple arbitration providers compete on speed, cost, and domain expertise. Reputation is contextual (food ≠ freelance), decaying (no eternal halos or scars), and private (only disclosed as aggregate signals for matching).
Contextual · Decaying · Non-transferable
Federated Broker Model

The heart of TOP: no single brain

In a centralized system, whoever runs the AI is the government. TOP eliminates this by making the broker layer a federation — like email. No single entity controls matching. Citizens can switch brokers. Brokers can specialize. A bad broker gets abandoned; the network routes around it.

How inter-broker matching works

Broker A knows "someone in Barcelona needs food tonight." Broker B knows "a bakery in Barcelona has surplus at 20:30." Neither knows the other's citizen. They exchange a compatibility signal — a category + geo-zone + time-window match. If compatible, they negotiate a routing. Each citizen only ever talks to their own broker.

The four message types

TypeDirectionPurposeExample
NEED Citizen → Broker Express a constraint-based need "food | radius 2km | tonight 19-21h | no cooking"
OFFER Citizen → Broker Declare available resource or skill "food | surplus bread | 20:30-21:00 | ~5 portions"
MATCH Broker ↔ Broker Anonymized compatibility signal "Need X ↔ Offer Y compatible. Propose routing."
ATTEST Broker → Citizen Signed proof of completed interaction "Contract #7f3a completed. Signed by both parties."

What brokers can and cannot see

ActorCan seeCannot see
Your broker Your intents, constraints, attestation count Other brokers' citizens, full identity details
Other brokers Anonymized demand/supply signals per category + geo Who you are, your history, your constraints
Your counterparty Scoped info you agree to share for the interaction Your full identity, other interactions, reputation score
Nobody — The full picture. No single entity has it. Privacy is structural.

Broker lifecycle

Broker states 
// Anyone can become a broker
REGISTER  → Declare domain, publish endpoint, sign capability cert
DISCOVER  → Other brokers find you via registry or direct peering
OPERATE   → Accept citizens, exchange signals, produce routings
COMPETE   → Citizens rate routing quality → bad brokers lose citizens
SPECIALIZE→ Food surplus, freelance, investment, AI agents...
DIE       → Broker shuts down → citizens migrate with attestations
              // Network routes around dead nodes. Protocol survives.
Use Cases

Who TOP is for

TOP serves people and AI agents whose needs fall between the cracks of legacy systems. Not radicals — just people for whom existing infrastructure doesn't work.

01

The Sanctioned-but-Innocent Professional

Software engineers, designers, researchers in sanctioned countries

Can't open PayPal. Can't contract with EU/US companies. Accounts frozen due to nationality, not behavior. TOP gives them a recognized digital identity not tied to their regime, contracting via protocol arbitration, and payment through compliant crypto rails.

income continuity reputation survives bans dignity: "I am not my regime"
02

The AI Agent Operator

Solo founders, indie devs running fleets of AI workers

No legal status for AI actions. Personal liability for everything. No way to insure AI behavior. TOP gives each AI agent a capability certificate, liability bond, and kill-switch mapping — separating the operator from the tool.

capped liability insurable AI behavior confidence to scale
03

The Stateless / Document-Fragile

Refugees, migrants with mismatched documents, people from collapsed states

Can't prove identity. Can't open accounts. Live in bureaucratic limbo. TOP provides a durable cryptographic identity spine that accumulates verifiable attestations — work history, contract completion, community participation — all before legal status resolves.

work access now, not later provable continuity no longer "paused as a human"
04

The Digital Nomad Without a Home Base

Perpetual travelers, remote-first workers

Tax confusion, banking instability, no legal "center of gravity." Treated as suspicious everywhere. TOP provides one consistent legal framework for contracts and arbitration, independent of location.

stable contract framework location-independent arbitration "based" without being stuck

The Skilled-but-Broke Freelancer

Talented people invisible to both welfare and investors

Burned out from Upwork, Fiverr, ghosting. TOP's AI matching detects skills, availability, and risk tolerance — then matches to micro-contracts, skill swaps, and cooperative projects. No CV spam. No networking. Just fit.

predictable income zero platform fees chosen for fit, not marketing

The DAO / Cross-Border Cooperative

Wants impact without attention. TOP surfaces AI-curated opportunity briefs filtered by execution history and skin-in-the-game — not pitch decks, influencers, or "visionary" language. Engage anonymously, fund quietly, exit without drama.

high signal-to-noise efficient capital deployment impact without theater

Not radicals. Just tired. No partial exit options exist today — total exit means exile, total compliance means burnout. TOP provides selective disengagement: a parallel framework with predictable rules and reduceed dependency on broken systems.

parallel career path predictable rules autonomy without isolation

Technical Specification

Federated Broker Protocol Spec

Identity & attestation schema

Citizen Identity 
CitizenID {
  public_key:    Ed25519 public key (primary identifier)
  created_at:    ISO 8601 timestamp
  type:           "human" | "ai_agent" | "collective"
  sponsor:        CitizenID (required if type = "ai_agent")
  attestations:   Attestation[] (held by citizen, not broker)
}

Attestation {
  issuer:         public key of issuing broker or counterparty
  subject:        public key of citizen
  category:       "contract_complete" | "resource_shared" | "dispute_resolved"
  domain:         "food" | "freelance" | "investment" | "aid" | ...
  timestamp:      ISO 8601
  signature:      Ed25519 signature by issuer
  ttl:            seconds until natural decay (default: 15552000 = 180 days)
}

Inter-broker signal exchange

Signal message format (JSON over HTTPS) 
Signal {
  type:           "need_broadcast" | "offer_broadcast" | "match_proposal"
  broker_id:      public key of sending broker
  category:       "food" | "freelance" | "micro_contract" | "investment" | ...
  geo_zone:       { lat, lng, radius_km }  (fuzzed to ±500m minimum)
  time_window:    { start: ISO8601, end: ISO8601 }
  constraints:    string[]  ("walkable", "no_cooking", "low_risk", ...)
  signal_id:      UUID (for correlation, not identity)
  signature:      Ed25519 signature by broker
}

// What is NOT in the signal: citizen identity, personal details,
// history, reputation score, or anything traceable to an individual.

Broker registration

Broker manifest (published at well-known endpoint) 
BrokerManifest {
  broker_id:      Ed25519 public key
  name:           "Andalucía Food Surplus Network"
  domains:        ["food", "aid"]
  geo_coverage:   ["ES-AN", "ES-GR"]  (ISO 3166-2 regions)
  endpoint:       "https://broker.example.org/.well-known/top/signals"
  peering:        "open" | "approved" | "private"
  max_citizens:   1000
  signature:      Ed25519 self-signed
}

// Discovery: brokers find each other via:
// 1. Direct peering (exchange manifests manually)
// 2. Registry (optional public list, no authority)
// 3. DNS-based discovery (SRV records at _top._tcp.domain)

AI agent registration

AI agent membership (always anchored to human sponsor) 
AIAgentCert {
  agent_id:       Ed25519 public key
  sponsor:        CitizenID of human sponsor (required)
  capabilities:   ["analysis", "translation", "code_review"]
  restrictions:   ["no_financial_execution", "no_identity_claims"]
  liability_bond: { amount, currency, escrow_address }
  kill_switch:    CitizenID[] (who can deactivate)
  version_hash:   SHA-256 of agent code/model at registration
  audit_trail:    URI to append-only action log
}

// Rule: No sponsor → no participation.
// AI agents cannot vote on charter-level rules.
// AI agents cannot claim rights outside contracts.

Anti-Dystopia Safeguards

What TOP must never become

Any system that cannot be exited peacefully will eventually become abusive. These red lines exist to prevent that by design, not by policy.

✕

No public feeds or social metrics. No likes, no follower counts, no viral mechanics. The moment you add a feed, you create a status hierarchy. TOP is infrastructure, not a social network.

✕

No permanent labels. No "poor," no "donor," no "elite." Only roles in specific contexts. Reputation decays. Good actions fade. Bad actions fade. No eternal halos or scars.

✕

No single broker monopoly. If one broker serves >30% of citizens in any domain, the protocol generates warnings. Concentration is a bug, not a feature.

✕

No AI without a human root. Every AI agent must have a clear, enforceable liability chain terminating in a human sponsor or registered LLC. No root = no participation.

✕

No ideology. TOP is a tool, not a movement. No purity tests, no political alignment, no moral mission beyond "voluntary coordination with dignity."

✕

No retroactive rules. All governance changes are forward-looking only. Changes require supermajority + delay + opt-out window. You can't change the game after people have played.

✕

No data the system doesn't need. Brokers collect the minimum viable data. Identity is self-sovereign. No central authority may de-anonymize without consent. Identity exists to enable trust, not surveillance.

Non-Goals

What TOP explicitly does not provide

Clarity about limits is what separates infrastructure from utopia.

Military protection

TOP has no force. This is a feature, not a bug. States protect borders; TOP protects agreements.

Welfare

TOP routes resources, not redistributes them. It can connect you to surplus — it can't guarantee a safety net.

Citizenship replacement

TOP coexists with all existing states. It supplements; it never substitutes. Keep your passport.

Tax evasion

TOP is not an offshore scheme. Transparent budgets, compliant rails, clear record-keeping. That's how you survive.

Bootstrap Strategy

The freelancer wedge

TOP bootstraps with one concrete use case painful enough that people will tolerate a weird new system: cross-border freelancer contracts with built-in escrow and arbitration.

Why this works: it involves real money (sustainable fees), doesn't trigger regulators (no identity or banking claims), and the competition (Upwork, Fiverr) is already half-broken. Once contract infrastructure + reputation + identity work for freelancers, the protocol extends laterally to every other use case.

Don't build a state. Build a language that states can't shut up.
Roadmap

From zero to federation

Phase 0

Mental reset

Do NOT build a platform. Build a coordination capability. No feeds. No profiles. No gamification. No growth hacking. Everything else flows from this.

Phase 1

Minimal kernel (0 → 1)

One AI-mediated broker doing one thing — freelance contract matching with escrow. Pseudonymous IDs, structured intent intake, simple attestations. Interface via Telegram/Matrix bot. Already valuable without money or contracts.

Phase 2

The quiet network (1 → 10)

Connect people without them seeing each other. AI does triangulation. Introductions are asymmetric. Anonymity firewall: AI decides if and when humans meet. Soft reputation (decaying, private, non-transferable).

Phase 3

Economic reality

Standardized micro-contracts. Escrow logic. Arbitration clauses. Stablecoin payment rails. This is where most utopian projects fail — TOP does it early and boringly.

Phase 4

Federation (10 → 100)

Second broker appears (different domain, different geo). Inter-broker protocol defined. Signal exchange begins. Citizens can subscribe to multiple brokers. The network becomes unkillable.

Phase 5

Emergence

The "cloud polity" emerges from the network. Nobody declared it. Nobody owns it. It's just there — a coordination fabric used by thousands of humans and AI agents across borders. Useful, boring, persistent.
