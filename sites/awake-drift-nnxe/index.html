Architecture

Concentric Rings Beneath a Lake

DeepCore places the entire nuclear island — reactor, steam generator, and condenser — in concentric toroidal rings excavated beneath an artificial lake. The surface shows nothing but water, floating solar panels, and a modest turbine building.

Each module is compact (20m outer diameter) and entirely self-contained. Steam rises to surface turbines through vertical shafts; condensate returns. The primary radioactive circuit never leaves the underground structure.

☢

Ring 1 — Reactor Core

Innermost ring (~3-4m diameter). Contains the reactor vessel, primary coolant loop, and control systems. Sealed beneath a reinforced containment dome.

♨

Ring 2 — Steam Generator

Middle ring (~10-12m diameter). Primary-to-secondary heat exchange. Radioactive water heats clean secondary water — no mixing, no contamination path.

❄

Ring 3 — Condenser

Outer ring (~18-20m diameter). Spent steam from turbines is cooled and condensed back to liquid water for recirculation. Enhanced mesh surfaces accelerate condensation.

⏻

Adaptive Cooling

Site-selectable cooling: passive lake, underground air-cooled condensers, evaporative tunnels, or cogeneration heat extraction. Same reactor, any climate.

⚡

Reactor Agnostic

Compatible with PWR, BWR, SMR, MSR, HTGR, and liquid-metal fast reactors. The architecture adapts — the physics stays proven.

♻

Integrated Fuel Cycle

Underground vaults for fresh fuel storage, spent fuel management, and optional pyrochemical recycling. No road transport of nuclear materials required.

Passive Safety

Designed to Fail Safely

Every major nuclear incident in history was a failure to maintain cooling. DeepCore inverts the problem: the reactor sits inside the water supply. In an emergency, water doesn't need to reach the reactor — it's already there.

Normal Operation

Sealed Underground

Reactor operates in sealed toroidal rings beneath the lake. No atmospheric interface.

Anomaly Detected

Automated Response

Redundant control systems detect temperature/pressure anomaly. Control rods insert automatically.

Loss of Cooling

Dome Breach Flooding

Sacrificial valves open. Lake water floods reactor chamber by gravity. No pumps needed.

Containment

Underground Sarcophagus

Contaminated water trapped in sealed toroidal rings. Zero atmospheric release. Self-entombing.

vs. Fukushima

Fukushima failed because pumps couldn't bring water to the reactor when power was lost. DeepCore's reactor is already submerged — flooding is gravity-driven, requiring zero power.

vs. Military Attack

All critical components are buried under meters of earth, water, and reinforced concrete. Even a direct strike creates no atmospheric contamination — the lake absorbs and contains.

vs. Sabotage

Physical access requires penetrating the lake, then sealed underground tunnels with restricted entry points. The architecture IS the security perimeter.

Thermal Management

Adaptive Cooling — One Architecture, Any Climate

The cooling subsystem is the only component that changes between deployments. The reactor, rings, dome, and safety systems remain identical.

🌊

Option A — Passive Lake

500-600m diameter lake with condenser spiral on bed. Evaporative surface cooling. Best for wet climates with reliable rainfall. Simplest and lowest cost.

🌀

Option B — Underground ACC

Air-cooled condenser chambers with natural/forced draft ventilation. Zero water consumption. Surface vents disguised as terrain. Best for arid/desert sites.

💧

Option C — Evaporative Tunnel

TBM-bored spiral tunnel with spray nozzles and forced air. Compact footprint, lake as water reservoir. Best for coastal sites with seawater access.

🏭

Option D — Cogeneration

Waste heat piped to district heating, desalination, greenhouses, aquaculture. Smallest lake needed. Best for urban/industrial deployments.

Target Markets

Who Needs Invisible, Indestructible Power?

🏳

Defense & Military

Hardened baseload for command centers, radar installations, and forward operating bases. The invisibility and attack resistance are primary differentiators no competitor offers.

🏝

Island Nations

Energy independence for Singapore, Cyprus, Malta, Pacific islands. Combined with desalination for simultaneous water and power security. Compact footprint suits limited land.

⚒

Remote Industry

Mining operations, Arctic stations, offshore platforms. Decades of autonomous operation with on-site fuel storage. No grid connection required.

🏙

Urban Cogeneration

Electricity + district heating beneath cities with zero visible infrastructure beyond a park lake. Particularly compelling for cold-climate dense cities.

Guaranteed 24/7 baseload for hyperscale computing. 50-200 MW modules match data center power needs precisely. On-site generation eliminates grid dependency.

National Grid Backbone

Dispatchable baseload complementing variable renewables. Clustered modules providing 100-250 MW with no public opposition from visual impact or safety fears.
